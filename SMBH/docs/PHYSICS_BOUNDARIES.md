# Physics and evidence boundaries

The SMBH Analogue Laboratory distinguishes experimental facts from auditory mappings.

## Paper-grounded values

Xu et al. report Lomb-Scargle optical-path peaks at `nL = 217 µm`, `237 µm`, and `268 µm`. Under waist-selective pumping all three are visible and the 217 µm family is localized at the photon sphere. Pumping near the outer edge leaves the 268 µm WGM peak. Uniform pumping is dominated by the 268 µm WGM, with weak PSM and left-edge WGM signatures. The narrow selective pump is 4 µm by 95 µm; the uniform rectangle is 50 µm by 95 µm.

The paper's numerical analysis shows that PSM Q factors are lower than the first-order WGM family, but may be comparable to or exceed higher-order WGMs. The app therefore does not treat `Q = 65` and `Q = 420` as measured values.

## Sonification choices

- placing the PSM family at 400 Hz;
- scaling other families by inverse optical-path ratios;
- representing a spectral family as an audible partial stack;
- choosing Q values for audible decay;
- turning separate pump experiments into a temporal montage;
- using stereo position, sustained sections, a narrative sequence, or cavity echo.

These choices are recorded in every recipe and manifest.

## Optical analogue versus astrophysical black hole

The paper uses *quasinormal mode* in the open-cavity optics sense: a leaky non-Hermitian resonance with complex frequency. The governing wave equation and preserved null geodesics support a rigorous structural analogy, but an exported audio file is not a measured LIGO waveform and the browser does not solve the full curved-surface eigenproblem, electromagnetic field, dye gain, or laser threshold dynamics.
